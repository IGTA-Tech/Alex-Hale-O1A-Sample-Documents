# VISA TYPE DETERMINATION

## Why O-1A Is Appropriate

The O-1A visa classification is designated for individuals with **extraordinary ability in the sciences, education, business, or athletics**. Alex Hale's case clearly falls within the athletics category, as his field of endeavor is professional American football placekicking.

### O-1A vs. P-1A Considerations

Mr. Hale currently holds P-1A status through his engagement with the Green Bay Packers. The transition to O-1A provides several strategic advantages:

| Factor | P-1A | O-1A |
|--------|------|------|
| **Petitioner Flexibility** | Must be employer/team | Any qualifying petitioner |
| **Employer Lock** | Tied to specific team | Greater flexibility |
| **Duration** | Event-based, up to 5 years | Up to 3 years, renewable |
| **Standard** | International recognition | Extraordinary ability |
| **Evidence Requirements** | Team-based recognition | Individual achievement focus |

**Rationale for O-1A Transition:**

1. **Petitioner Independence**: The petition is being filed by Innovative Global Accelerator Studios LLC, providing Mr. Hale with greater career flexibility than team-specific P-1A status.

2. **Individual Recognition Focus**: Mr. Hale's individual achievements (Lou Groza semifinalist, Academic All-America, etc.) are more naturally suited to the O-1A individual extraordinary ability framework than the P-1A team-recognition structure.

3. **Career Versatility**: O-1A status allows Mr. Hale to pursue opportunities beyond NFL team employment, including:
   - Kicking coaching and instruction
   - Athletic training and consulting
   - Sports media and commentary
   - Athletic performance businesses

4. **Recognition of Complete Achievement Profile**: The O-1A criteria allow for a holistic presentation of Mr. Hale's achievements across awards, media coverage, critical roles, and original contributions—a more comprehensive picture than P-1A's narrower focus.

# REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

## Statutory Basis

The O-1A classification is established under **INA § 101(a)(15)(O)(i)**, which provides for the admission of:

> "An alien who has extraordinary ability in the sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics which has been demonstrated by sustained national or international acclaim and whose achievements have been recognized in the field through extensive documentation..."

## Regulatory Requirements

Under **8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)**, a petition for an O-1A alien of extraordinary ability in athletics must be accompanied by evidence that the alien has received sustained national or international acclaim. The regulation specifies that such evidence **shall include evidence of at least three (3) of the following**:

### The Eight O-1A Criteria

| Criterion | CFR Citation | Description |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **A** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(A) | Receipt of nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence |
| **B** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(B) | Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievements |
| **C** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(C) | Published material about the beneficiary in major media |
| **D** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(D) | Participation as a judge of the work of others |
| **E** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(E) | Original contributions of major significance |
| **F** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(F) | Authorship of scholarly articles |
| **G** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(G) | Employment in critical or essential capacity for distinguished organizations |
| **H** | 8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(H) | High salary or remuneration |

## Comparable Evidence Provision

Under **8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(v)**, if the above criteria do not readily apply to the beneficiary's occupation, the petitioner may submit **comparable evidence** to establish eligibility. This provision is particularly relevant for athletes whose achievements may not fit traditional academic or business frameworks.

## Applicable Precedent

**Kazarian v. USCIS, 596 F.3d 1115 (9th Cir. 2010)** established the two-step analysis for extraordinary ability petitions:

1. **Step One**: Determine whether the evidence submitted meets at least three of the regulatory criteria
2. **Step Two**: Conduct a final merits determination to evaluate whether the totality of the evidence demonstrates sustained national or international acclaim and extraordinary ability
