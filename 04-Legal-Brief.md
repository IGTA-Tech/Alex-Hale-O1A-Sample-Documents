# III. LEGAL STANDARDS

## A. Statutory Requirements

The O-1A nonimmigrant classification is established under **Section 101(a)(15)(O)(i) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA)**, which provides for the admission of:

> "An alien who has extraordinary ability in the sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics which has been demonstrated by sustained national or international acclaim and whose achievements have been recognized in the field through extensive documentation..."

## B. Regulatory Criteria

Under **8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(iii)**, a petition for an alien of extraordinary ability in athletics must include evidence demonstrating sustained national or international acclaim. The regulation provides that such evidence **shall include at least three (3) of the following**:

- [ ] **(A)** Documentation of the beneficiary's receipt of nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence in the field of endeavor

- [ ] **(B)** Documentation of the beneficiary's membership in associations in the field for which classification is sought, which require outstanding achievements of their members, as judged by recognized national or international experts

- [ ] **(C)** Published material in professional or major trade publications or major media about the beneficiary, relating to the beneficiary's work in the field

- [ ] **(D)** Evidence of the beneficiary's participation on a panel, or individually, as a judge of the work of others in the same or allied field

- [ ] **(E)** Evidence of the beneficiary's original scientific, scholarly, or business-related contributions of major significance in the field

- [ ] **(F)** Evidence of the beneficiary's authorship of scholarly articles in the field, in professional journals, or other major media

- [ ] **(G)** Evidence that the beneficiary has been employed in a critical or essential capacity for organizations and establishments that have a distinguished reputation

- [ ] **(H)** Evidence that the beneficiary has either commanded a high salary or will command a high salary or other remuneration for services

## C. Standard of Proof

The standard of proof in immigration proceedings is "preponderance of the evidence," meaning the Petitioner must establish that the claim is "more likely than not" or "probably true." *Matter of Chawathe*, 25 I&N Dec. 369, 376 (AAO 2010).

## D. Applicable USCIS Policy Guidance

USCIS Policy Manual, Volume 2, Part M, Chapter 4 provides guidance on the O-1A classification. The Policy Manual clarifies that an officer should evaluate each criterion independently and determine whether the evidence submitted meets the plain language requirements of the regulation.

## E. Comparable Evidence Standard

Under **8 CFR § 214.2(o)(3)(v)**, if the criteria described in the regulations do not readily apply to the beneficiary's occupation, the petitioner may submit **comparable evidence** to establish the beneficiary's eligibility. This provision acknowledges that evidence of extraordinary ability may take different forms depending on the field.
